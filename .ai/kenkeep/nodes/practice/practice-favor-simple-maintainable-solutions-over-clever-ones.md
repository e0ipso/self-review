Prefer simple solutions first: the most straightforward approach that meets requirements wins over a complex "perfect" one. Avoid over-engineering — don't create complex systems when simple ones work.

Use established patterns rather than inventing new ones, and keep code structure obvious and predictable. Add external dependencies only when essential, but don't re-invent the wheel either.

A working simple solution is better than a complex "perfect" one.
